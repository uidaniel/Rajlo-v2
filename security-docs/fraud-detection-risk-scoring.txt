RAJLO Fraud Detection & Risk Scoring System Specification
Effective Date: May 17, 2026
This specification defines the fraud detection, account risk scoring, suspicious activity monitoring, automated enforcement, and fraud investigation systems for RAJLO.
Purpose:
Detect and reduce fraud
Protect rider and driver accounts
Reduce chargebacks and payment abuse
Detect fake GPS activity
Detect fake rides and referral abuse
Protect payouts and platform revenue
Support operational enforcement
Preserve legal evidence
Improve platform trust and safety
This system applies to all riders, drivers, admins, moderators, payment systems, onboarding systems, and backend operational services.

1. Core Fraud Threats
The system should monitor and detect:
1.1 Rider Fraud
Fake trip requests
Chargeback abuse
Stolen payment methods
Multiple fake accounts
Promotional abuse
Refund abuse
Identity manipulation
Account sharing
1.2 Driver Fraud
Fake GPS activity
Fake trip completion
Trip inflation
Shared driver accounts
Identity fraud
Referral abuse
Artificial surge manipulation
Driver collusion
1.3 Platform Fraud
Device farming
Coordinated fraud rings
API abuse
Automated bots
Scripted activity
Multiple-account abuse
Backend exploitation attempts

2. Risk Scoring System
Every rider and driver should have an internal dynamic risk score.
Recommended score range:
0–100
Examples:
0–20 = Low risk
21–50 = Moderate risk
51–75 = High risk
76–100 = Critical risk
Risk scores should update automatically based on:
Payment behavior
GPS behavior
Complaint history
Device activity
Login patterns
Fraud indicators
Chargebacks
Cancellation behavior
Multiple-account detection

3. GPS Spoofing Detection
The backend should monitor for:
Impossible travel speeds
GPS jumps
Emulator detection
Fake GPS applications
Device location inconsistencies
Trip routes inconsistent with road networks
Frequent GPS anomalies
Recommended actions:
Flag account
Increase risk score
Trigger manual review
Restrict payouts
Suspend account

4. Device Fingerprinting
The system should collect and compare:
Device IDs
Browser fingerprints
App version
OS version
IP addresses where available
Login history
Session metadata
Purpose:
Detect multiple-account abuse
Detect shared accounts
Detect fraud rings
Detect suspicious device reuse

5. Payment Fraud Detection
The system should monitor:
Chargebacks
Failed payment attempts
Stolen card indicators
High refund frequency
Unusual payment behavior
Reused payment methods across accounts
Rapid account creation + payment activity
High-risk payment activity should trigger:
Payment restrictions
Ride restrictions
Manual review
Temporary account suspension

6. Referral Abuse Detection
The system should detect:
Self-referrals
Device-linked referral abuse
Fake account farming
Referral loops
Artificial trip generation for bonuses
Recommended controls:
Referral payout delays
Device/IP comparison
Minimum trip requirements
Fraud review queues

7. Behavioral Monitoring
Monitor for suspicious behavior patterns such as:
Excessive cancellations
Rapid account switching
Frequent login device changes
Unusual ride patterns
Abnormal payout behavior
Unusual GPS routes
Repeated failed verification attempts
Behavior anomalies should influence risk scoring.

8. Automated Enforcement Rules
The system should support automatic:
Account flagging
Ride restrictions
Driver go-online restrictions
Payout holds
Temporary suspensions
Referral freezes
Fraud investigation escalations
Critical fraud triggers may justify immediate suspension.

9. Manual Review Queue
High-risk accounts should enter a manual review queue.
Admins/moderators should be able to review:
Device history
GPS history
Payment history
Complaint history
Linked accounts
Fraud indicators
Risk score timeline

11. Fraud Investigation Tools
Admins should be able to:
View linked accounts
View risk scores
View fraud flags
Freeze payouts
Restrict accounts
Escalate investigations
Export fraud evidence
View GPS anomalies
Review payment disputes

12. Payout Protection Rules
High-risk driver activity may trigger:
Delayed payouts
Manual payout review
Fraud investigation hold
Temporary payout freezes
The system should preserve evidence before releasing disputed payouts.

13. Security Requirements
Fraud systems should be protected with:
Admin-only access
Role-based permissions
Immutable audit logs
Backend encryption where possible
Secure investigation exports
Risk event retention
Fraud data should not be publicly accessible.

14. Evidence Retention
Preserve:
GPS snapshots
Device fingerprints
Payment logs
Risk score history
Fraud flags
Investigation history
Enforcement actions
Historical fraud evidence should remain accessible for:
Legal defense
Chargeback disputes
Insurance matters
Regulatory investigations

15. Recommended API Endpoints
Get Risk Score
GET /admin/fraud/risk-score/{userId}
View Fraud Flags
GET /admin/fraud/flags/{userId}
Freeze Payout
POST /admin/fraud/payout-freeze/{driverId}
Open Fraud Investigation
POST /admin/fraud/investigations
View Linked Accounts
GET /admin/fraud/linked-accounts/{userId}

16. Admin Dashboard Requirements
Dashboard should display:
High-risk users
Fraud investigation queue
GPS spoofing alerts
Chargeback alerts
Linked account alerts
Suspicious payout activity
Risk score trends
Admins should be able to:
Suspend users
Freeze payouts
Escalate cases
Export evidence
Resolve investigations

Demonstrate operational diligence

Fraud indicators detected

Actions taken
Evidence preserved

Daniel should implement a fraud detection and risk scoring system that:
