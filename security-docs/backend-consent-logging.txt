RAJLO Backend Consent Logging Specification
Effective Date: May 17, 2026
Purpose: Legal acceptance, policy versioning, audit evidence, and user consent enforcement for RAJLO.
This specification defines how RAJLO should record, store, verify, and enforce user consent for all legal policies, permissions, payment authorizations, GPS disclosures, and platform agreements.

1. Primary Goal
RAJLO must be able to prove that each rider and driver clearly accepted the correct version of each required legal document before using the platform.
The backend should store tamper-resistant consent records showing:
Who accepted
What they accepted
Which policy version they accepted
When they accepted it
Where they accepted it from
What device/browser/app version was used
What IP address was used, where available
Whether re-acceptance is required after updates
This is critical for legal protection, payment disputes, account disputes, GPS tracking disputes, and enforcement actions.

2. Legal Documents Requiring Acceptance
The backend must support consent logging for the following RAJLO documents:
Terms of Service
Privacy Policy
Driver Agreement
Driver Cancellation & Service Reliability Policy
Rider Cancellation, Conduct & Liability Policy
Payment & Refund Policy
Community Guidelines
Safety Disclaimer & Emergency Policy
Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
Driver Earnings Disclaimer & Independent Income Disclosure
Website & App Disclaimer

3. User Types

3.1 Rider Required Documents
Riders must accept:
Terms of Service
Privacy Policy
Rider Cancellation, Conduct & Liability Policy
Payment & Refund Policy
Community Guidelines
Safety Disclaimer & Emergency Policy
Acceptable Use Policy
Website & App Disclaimer
3.2 Driver Required Documents
Drivers must accept:
Terms of Service
Privacy Policy
Driver Agreement
Driver Cancellation & Service Reliability Policy
Payment & Refund Policy
Community Guidelines
Safety Disclaimer & Emergency Policy
Acceptable Use Policy
Driver Earnings Disclaimer & Independent Income Disclosure
Website & App Disclaimer

4. Consent Events to Log
The backend must log these consent events:
4.1 Account Creation Consent
When a rider or driver creates an account, log acceptance of required policies.
4.2 Login Re-Acceptance Consent
If policies have changed, force the user to re-accept before continuing.
4.3 GPS Permission Consent
Log that the user acknowledged location tracking terms.
For drivers, this must include live/background location tracking while online or during trips.
For riders, this must include pickup/drop-off location processing and trip tracking.
4.4 Payment Authorization Consent
Log that the rider authorized RAJLO to process:
Trip fares
Cancellation fees
No-show fees
Outstanding balances
Refund adjustments
Automatic charges to saved payment methods
4.5 OTP Verification Consent
Log that the user agreed to receive OTP messages for authentication and account security.
4.6 Driver Online Consent
Before a driver can go online for the first time, the backend must verify that all driver-required documents are accepted.
4.7 Rider Trip Request Consent
Before a rider can request a trip, the backend must verify that all rider-required documents are accepted.

6. Backend API Endpoints
6.1 Get Active Legal Documents
GET /legal/documents?role=rider
GET /legal/documents?role=driver
Returns active documents required for the user role.
Response should include:
document_key
title
version
effective_date
content_url
content_hash
requires_acceptance
6.2 Submit Consent
POST /legal/consents
Request body:
{
"userId": "user_123",
"role": "rider",
"acceptedDocuments": [
{
"documentKey": "terms_of_service",
"version": "2026.05.17",
"contentHash": "sha256_hash_here"
}
],
"acceptanceMethod": "signup_checkbox",
"sourceScreen": "rider_signup",
"checkboxLabel": "I agree to RAJLO's Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, and required platform policies.",
"consentTextSnapshot": "By continuing, I agree to RAJLO's Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Payment & Refund Policy, Community Guidelines, Safety Disclaimer, Acceptable Use Policy, and other applicable platform policies."
}
Backend should automatically append:
accepted_at
ip_address
user_agent
device_id
app_version
platform
6.3 Check Consent Status
GET /legal/consents/status?userId=user_123&role=rider
Returns:
is_compliant
missing_documents
accepted_documents
reacceptance_required
6.4 Force Policy Re-Acceptance
POST /admin/legal/require-reacceptance
Admin-only endpoint used when policy versions are updated.
Request body:
{
"documentKey": "privacy_policy",
"newVersion": "2026.06.01",
"rolesAffected": ["rider", "driver"]
}
6.5 Consent Audit Export
GET /admin/legal/consents/export?userId=user_123
Admin-only endpoint for legal, disputes, chargebacks, and investigations.
Export should include:
User identity
Documents accepted
Versions accepted
Timestamps
IP address
Device/app details
Consent text snapshots
Audit logs

7. Frontend UX Requirements
7.1 Rider Signup Screen
Must show a required checkbox:
“I agree to RAJLO’s Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Rider Cancellation Policy, Payment & Refund Policy, Community Guidelines, Safety Disclaimer, Acceptable Use Policy, and Website & App Disclaimer.”
User cannot create account unless checked.
7.2 Driver Signup Screen
Must show a required checkbox:
“I agree to RAJLO’s Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Driver Agreement, Driver Cancellation Policy, Payment & Refund Policy, Community Guidelines, Safety Disclaimer, Acceptable Use Policy, Driver Earnings Disclaimer, and Website & App Disclaimer.”
User cannot complete driver registration unless checked.
7.3 Location Consent Screen
Must show before requesting location permissions:
“RAJLO uses location data to match rides, track trips, improve pickup accuracy, support safety features, prevent fraud, and operate real-time transportation services. Drivers may be tracked while online or during trips, including background location where permitted by device settings.”
7.4 Payment Authorization Screen
Must show before adding payment method or requesting first trip:
“By continuing, I authorize RAJLO and its payment processors to electronically charge my saved payment method for trip fares, cancellation fees, no-show fees, outstanding balances, and applicable platform charges.”
7.5 Policy Update Screen
If policies change, user must see:
“RAJLO has updated its legal policies. To continue using the platform, please review and accept the updated documents.”
User cannot continue until accepted.

8. Backend Enforcement Rules
8.1 Rider Enforcement
A rider cannot:
Request a ride
Add payment method
Complete booking
Use live trip services
unless rider consent status is compliant.
8.2 Driver Enforcement
A driver cannot:
Complete onboarding
Go online
Accept trips
Receive payouts
unless driver consent status is compliant.
8.3 Missing Consent Handling
If required consent is missing, backend returns:
403 Forbidden
{
"error": "LEGAL_CONSENT_REQUIRED",
"message": "Updated legal consent is required before continuing.",
"missingDocuments": ["privacy_policy", "payment_refund_policy"]
}

9. Policy Versioning Rules
Each legal document must have:
Version number
Effective date
Content hash
Active/inactive status
Applies-to role
Recommended version format:
YYYY.MM.DD
Example:
2026.05.17
When a document changes materially:
Create a new version
Mark re-acceptance required
Store old version permanently
Do not overwrite historical content

10. Evidence Preservation Rules
Do not delete consent records.
Consent records should be retained for legal, regulatory, fraud, payment, and dispute purposes.
Store:
Timestamp
Document version
User role
Consent text snapshot
Device/app data
IP address where available
Content hash
Historical consent records must remain available even if a user account is deactivated.

11. Security Requirements
Consent logs should be protected from unauthorized modification.
Recommended protections:
Admin-only access
Audit trail for changes
No hard deletion
Append-only log behavior where possible
Database backups
Role-based permissions
Encryption at rest where available
Secure API authentication

12. Admin Dashboard Requirements
Admin should be able to view:
User consent status
Missing documents
Accepted document versions
Acceptance timestamps
Device/app information
IP address where available
Re-acceptance requirements
Admin should be able to:
Export consent evidence
Trigger policy re-acceptance
View policy version history
Mark documents as active/inactive

13. Minimum Production Acceptance Checklist
Before launch, confirm:
All legal documents have version numbers
All legal documents have active URLs
Signup requires checkbox acceptance
Driver onboarding requires Driver Agreement acceptance
Rider trip request checks legal consent
Driver go-online checks legal consent
GPS disclosure is shown before location permission
Payment authorization is logged before first charge
Consent records store timestamps
Consent records store policy versions
Consent records store content hashes
Consent records store device/app data
Admin can export consent logs
Backend blocks users missing required consent
Re-acceptance works after policy updates

14. Developer Summary
Daniel should implement a backend legal consent system that:
Stores every RAJLO legal document with versioning.
Requires riders and drivers to accept the proper documents.
Logs exact acceptance events with timestamp, policy version, device, app version, IP, and consent text.
Blocks riders from requesting trips if consent is incomplete.
Blocks drivers from going online if consent is incomplete.
Forces re-acceptance when policies are updated.
Preserves historical consent records permanently for disputes and legal evidence.
Provides admin export tools for legal, chargeback, fraud, or safety investigations.
This system is a critical legal protection layer for RAJLO because it proves that users agreed to the platform terms before using the service.
